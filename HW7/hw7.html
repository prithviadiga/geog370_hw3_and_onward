Homework 7: Make a choropleth or proportional symbol map

My map is a view of the city limits of Seattle, divided into Land Tracts. A choropleth layer showing the percent of residents that live under the poverty line has
been added. I chose to use orange to represent poverty so as to avoid political connotations while simulatenously having negative connotations and catching the viewer's eye.
I added two scales in both feet and miles because the area represented is relatively small and so both methods could be relatively useful. Creating the map involved finding
non-census data sources. It was especially difficult to find CSV data sources because they have so many non-GIS uses. Eventually I found data.gov, which is an open source
government database. I used filters to specify that I wanted CSV GIS data and found that the city of Seattle has lots of data listed on the site. I found a shapefile of
land tracts in order to have physical divisions within which to place the choropleth boundaries and then started looking for an appropriate CSV. I chose to focus my map on
poverty since there is currently a cost of living crisis. The CSV had roughly 1700 GEOIDs while the Land Tract shp file had 135, and so I cleaned up the CSV by hand and joined
it to the shp file, and then created a new field in order to display the percetage ratio. I then used natural breaks in order to display the map divisions and exported it as a png.
